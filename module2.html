Contract Specifications: MES & MNQ

Micro E-mini futures are 1/10th the size of standard E-mini contracts, making them ideal for algorithmic strategy development and smaller accounts without sacrificing real-market exposure.

Intraday margin requirements vary by broker and can be as low as $40/contract with some discount futures brokers. Always confirm with your specific broker before sizing positions.

Session Hours & Market Structure

Automated strategies must respect session filters to avoid trading in low-liquidity overnight gaps. The most reliable automated trading windows on MES/MNQ are:

SessionTime (CT)Characteristics
Pre-Market7:00 AM – 9:30 AMBuilding volume, news-driven moves
NY Open9:30 AM – 11:00 AMHighest volume, widest range, ideal for momentum strategies
Midday Lull11:00 AM – 1:00 PMLow volume, choppy — avoid scalping
Afternoon Session1:00 PM – 3:00 PMSecondary volume window, trending behavior
Close3:00 PM – 4:00 PMClosing imbalances, final directional push
Overnight4:00 PM – 9:30 AMThin liquidity, wider spreads — avoid unless strategy is specifically designed for it
⚠️

Strategy Filter: Always add a session filter in your NinjaScript. Trading the overnight session with a scalping strategy designed for the NY open will dramatically reduce performance.

Reading Order Flow & Market Structure

While automated strategies use technical signals, understanding the underlying market mechanics improves your signal design and helps you avoid common failure modes.

Key Concepts

  • Delta: Net difference between buy and sell volume at each price level. Positive delta = buying pressure. Negative delta = selling pressure.
  • Imbalance: A significant mismatch between bids and offers at a price level, often predictive of short-term directional movement.
  • Point of Control (POC): The price level with the highest traded volume in a given session — acts as a magnet for price.
  • Value Area: The range where ~70% of volume traded. Price outside value area often reverts back — useful for mean-reversion strategies.
  • Market Profile: Distribution of volume by price over time. D-shaped profile = balanced/rotational market. B or P shape = trending market.
✅

You don't need to trade manually with order flow to benefit from these concepts. Understanding delta and value area helps you design better entry filters for automated strategies — particularly for mean-reversion bots.

Risk Sizing for Micro Futures Accounts

Position sizing is the single most important factor in automated trading survival. The standard rule: risk no more than 1–2% of account equity per trade.

Example: $5,000 Account

ParameterMES ExampleMNQ Example
Account Size$5,000$5,000
Risk Per Trade (1%)$50$50
Stop Loss20 ticks20 ticks
Tick Value$1.25/tick$0.50/tick
Risk Per Contract at Stop$25.00$10.00
Max Contracts2 contracts5 contracts
⚠️

Never risk more than 2% per trade. At 2% risk with a 50% win rate and 1:1.5 R:R, your account survives a 20-trade losing streak and still has 60%+ of capital. Exceeding this threshold is the #1 account killer in automated scalping.

Commission Impact on Scalping Strategies

Commissions are a silent killer of scalping strategies. A backtest ignoring commissions can show a strategy is profitable, when in live trading it's a net loser.

Typical Commission Costs (Round-Turn)

Broker TypePer SideRound TurnMES Tick EquivalentMNQ Tick Equivalent
Discount Futures Broker$0.25–$0.50$0.50–$1.000.4–0.8 ticks1.0–2.0 ticks
Standard Broker$1.00–$2.00$2.00–$4.001.6–3.2 ticks4.0–8.0 ticks
⚠️

Always enable commissions in Strategy Analyzer. Set realistic per-side values matching your broker. A scalping strategy targeting 6–8 ticks with a $4.00 round-turn commission is consuming 50–67% of each winner in fees on MNQ.

✅

Rule of thumb: Your average winner should be at least 3–4× the round-turn commission. On MNQ at $1.00 round-turn, aim for at minimum 6–8 tick average wins.
