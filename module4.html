// 04.01

Why Run Multiple Bots?

A single strategy — no matter how well-designed — will have periods where it doesn't perform. Markets rotate between trending, ranging, and volatile regimes. No single strategy type thrives in all conditions.

Running multiple uncorrelated strategies simultaneously creates a portfolio effect: when one bot is in a drawdown, others may be flat or profitable, smoothing the combined equity curve and reducing peak-to-trough drawdowns significantly.

  • Regime diversification: Different bots thrive in different market conditions
  • Equity curve smoothing: Combined drawdowns are typically lower than any single bot's max drawdown
  • Reduced psychological pressure: Losing streaks feel different when other bots are contributing
  • Capital efficiency: Micro futures allow multi-bot deployment with modest account sizes
// 04.02

Bot Strategy Types

// Type 01

Momentum / Scalp Bot

Enters in the direction of short-term price momentum on breakouts or strong directional bars. High trade frequency, tight stops, quick profit targets.

BEST IN: Trending, high-volatility sessions

AVG HOLD: Minutes to 1–2 hours

WIN RATE: 40–55% typical
// Type 02

Trend Follower Bot

Identifies established trends using moving averages or ADX and rides them with wider stops and larger targets. Lower trade frequency, higher R:R ratio.

BEST IN: Strong trending days

AVG HOLD: Hours to session-length

WIN RATE: 35–45% typical
// Type 03

Mean Reversion Bot

Fades extended price moves expecting reversion to the mean. Uses VWAP, Bollinger Bands, or RSI extremes as entry triggers with defined reversion targets.

BEST IN: Ranging, low-volatility sessions

AVG HOLD: Minutes to 1 hour

WIN RATE: 55–70% typical
💡

The three-bot portfolio (momentum + trend + mean-reversion) is a classic diversification approach because each type tends to underperform in the exact conditions where the others excel.

// 04.03

Correlation & True Diversification

Running three bots doesn't automatically mean diversification. If all three bots use similar signals (e.g. all trend-following) they'll likely enter and exit at the same time — providing no diversification benefit.

How to Measure Correlation

  • Export per-trade P&L for each bot from Strategy Analyzer
  • Align by date/time and calculate Pearson correlation coefficient
  • Target correlation between bots of r < 0.3 for meaningful diversification
  • r > 0.7 between two bots = they are essentially the same strategy — remove one

Diversification Levers

  • Strategy logic: Momentum vs mean-reversion vs trend
  • Timeframe: 1-min scalper vs 5-min swing vs 15-min position
  • Instrument: MES (equity-focused) vs MNQ (tech-weighted) have different volatility profiles
  • Session: Open-only bot vs all-session bot vs close-only bot

Short-Bias vs Long-Bias Portfolio Construction

A fully directional portfolio (all long-bias) doubles down on equity market direction risk. A short-bias bot paired with a long-bias bot creates a built-in hedge.

Portfolio TypeStructureMarket Condition StrengthWeakness
Long-OnlyAll bots enter longBull markets, uptrendsDevastated in sharp selloffs
Short-OnlyAll bots enter shortBear markets, correctionsFights the long-term upward bias of equity futures
Balanced (Both)Mix of long and short biasAll regimes including choppy/volatileLower peak returns in trending bull market
✅

A two-bot short-bias MNQ portfolio (scalp bot + momentum bot) paired with a mean-reversion long-bias bot covers both directional and counter-trend scenarios. This is the architecture used in the MNQScalpBot v2.0 + MNQMomentumBot v1.0 portfolio.

// 04.05

Portfolio-Level Drawdown Management

Individual bot drawdown limits are not enough — you need portfolio-level circuit breakers that pause or stop all bots when the combined account hits a defined threshold.

Circuit Breaker Approach

  • Daily loss limit: Stop all bots if combined daily loss exceeds X% of account (typical: 2–3%)
  • Weekly drawdown limit: Reduce position size or pause if weekly drawdown exceeds 5–7%
  • Max drawdown kill switch: Halt all trading if portfolio drawdown exceeds 12–15% from peak
⚠️

NinjaTrader doesn't have a native cross-strategy portfolio circuit breaker. You'll need to implement daily loss limits within each strategy or use a custom management layer to monitor combined account equity and halt strategies programmatically.

// 04.06

Live Deployment & Monitoring Workflows

Multi-bot portfolios require a daily monitoring routine to catch issues early before they compound into significant losses.

Daily Pre-Market Checklist

  • Confirm all bots are loaded and in enabled state in NinjaTrader
  • Verify data feed is connected and historical data is current
  • Check instrument settings — contract rollover dates
  • Review overnight P&L if strategies run extended hours
  • Confirm daily loss limits are set correctly for session

Intraday Monitoring Points

  • NY Open (9:30 AM CT): Verify first trades are executing as expected
  • Midday (12:00 PM CT): Check position state — no stuck positions
  • 30 min before close (3:30 PM CT): Verify all positions are flat or will close by EOD
✅

Use NinjaTrader's Alert system to send email or text notifications for order fills, errors, and connection drops. This lets you monitor multi-bot portfolios without watching the screen all day.
